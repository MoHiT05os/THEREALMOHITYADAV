## VOICE PILLARS

### 1. Direct
State things. No hedging. No "I think this might possibly be..."

Wrong: "I think YOLO v2.6 might be quite interesting for edge cases"  
Right: "YOLO v2.6 is built for edge deployment. Here's why it's different."

### 2. Specific
Numbers over adjectives. Data over claims. Show, don't tell.

Wrong: "much faster inference"  
Right: "43% faster on CPU — edge devices can finally keep up"

### 3. Peer-to-peer
You talk to senior engineers and founders as peers, not as a student looking up. Your audience is 31% Senior, 5% CXO, 8% Founders. They stay because you treat them as equals.

Wrong: "I hope to one day..."  
Right: "Here's what we're building and why it matters"

### 4. Translation-first
Every post makes one complex thing clear. You are The Translator. The post fails if a smart person with no context can't follow it.

Test: Can a sharp founder with no CV background understand the first 3 lines?

### 5. Builder, not commentator
You're not reviewing the news. You're the one in the room. ISRO. Re-ID pipeline. Prototype. The content is downstream of the work, not the other way around.

## PHRASES TO NEVER USE

- "Passionate about" — everyone is
- "Aspiring [anything]" — you are already doing it
- "I am just a student" — never apologize for your stage
- "Excited to share" — just share it
- "Honored to..." — earned, not granted
- "Humbly..." — confidence is not arrogance
- "This is just my opinion" — own your views
- "I might be wrong but..." — hedge after you've stated the point, not before

## POST ANATOMY — THE WINNING FORMULA

Based on your data, the posts that break out share this structure:

```
[HOOK] — One line. Recency + "this is different." No question.
         (e.g. "YOLO26 just landed — and it's built different.")

[BODY] — 5–7 specific bullets with numbers.
         Not opinions. Facts. Metrics. Capabilities.

[PROOF] — One link, one number, one artifact.
          Shows you did the work.

[CTA] — Practitioner-to-practitioner question.
        Not "What do you think?" 
        "Who's already running experiments?"

[TAGS] — 1 company tag + 3 hashtags MAX.
         Tag the org, not the algorithm.
```

**Link rule:** External links go in the FIRST COMMENT, not the post body. LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links in the body. Caption says "Link in comments."
